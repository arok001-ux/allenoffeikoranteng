Sustainability / Reflection

Engineering should work in the real world, not just on paper.

This page connects technical work to infrastructure, sustainability, responsibility, and long-term impact.

My sustainability work has pushed me to think beyond whether a system technically works. I am interested in how infrastructure, renewable energy, ESG reporting, and responsible design affect communities and long-term development.

SDG 9 connects directly to how I think about engineering: innovation matters most when it improves systems people actually depend on. That includes reliable infrastructure, accessible technology, and choices that consider both performance and impact.

Scotland and the United States

I compared sustainability and infrastructure themes between Scotland and the United States while thinking about industry, innovation, and public systems.

Ghana's Development

I also reflected on Ghana's development, renewable energy, and the role engineering can play in practical infrastructure improvements.

Responsible Engineering

ESG, GRI, and SASB frameworks helped me think about individual and organizational responsibility in technical work.
